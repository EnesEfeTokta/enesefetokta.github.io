My Journey

My Story

I'm at the Beginning, but My Direction Is Clear

The Journey Begins

My introduction to software didn't start as a career choice, but as a need to solve problems. Over time, that need evolved into a habit of systematic thinking, building, and continuous learning.

Today, my focus is on learning processes, lessons learned from mistakes, and solutions that have real-world impact. I don't see coding as a purely technical activity; to me, it's the art of asking the right questions, designing sustainable systems, and creating meaningful value.

In the short term, my goal is to build a strong technical foundation. In the long term, I aim to create impact on an international scale. My direction is clear: think better, build cleaner, and grow by sharing.

A Transparent Journey

What I build and document here is not an "expert showcase," but a transparent development journal. If my experiences can offer even a small contribution to someone reading, then I know I'm on the right path.

Education & Roots

I completed my high school education at Şemsi Türkmen Anatolian High School. Even with limited resources, I started developing games with Unity. Testing my first game, Witch Land, on my classmates' phones was the first time I felt the power of creation.

I am currently continuing my education at Atatürk University. University changed my core perspective, leading me deeper into the .NET ecosystem and modern development with JavaScript and TypeScript.

Beyond the Screen

Life is more than just code. While in my third year at university, I share my life with Oğlum, my first pet cat. Software taught me how to build systems; living with him taught me responsibility, patience, and empathy. I believe staying human is just as important as technical growth.

My Philosophy

My life goal is not to become a perfect developer, but to become a happy and successful human being. During my visit to Estonia, I made a promise to myself:

"Enes, work for yourself. Work for your happiness."

I still carry that sentence with me like a compass, guiding every decision I make in my career and personal life.

The Best Is Yet To Come

With the hope that the future Enes will be more productive, more successful, and happier.
